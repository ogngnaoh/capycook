## Non-blocking nits

1. **Imprecise "architecture rec 4" citation tag for the live-retrieval-to-P1 decision.** Memo rec 3 and v0.3 changelog item 3 both cite "architecture rec 4" as a driver for moving live-literature retrieval to P1. But architecture's actual recommendation 4 reads: *"Front-load the live-literature retrieval pipeline in the schedule... don't budget all three sources as equal-effort"* — i.e., it argues for prioritizing/front-loading live retrieval inside P0, not deferring it to P1. The mislabel originates in `05-critique.md`'s own de-risk note ("...architecture rec 4") and is faithfully propagated by both downstream documents, so it is not an invented citation — a real, traceable recommendation exists at that location — but it argues the opposite direction. This does not weaken the underlying decision, which is independently and correctly grounded in critique's own [High] top concern #2 and market's rec 4 ("De-risk the hybrid-retrieval P0 dependency... Sequence live scholarly retrieval as a P1 layer"), both accurately cited alongside it. Cheap fix: change the citation to "critique [High]; market rec 4" only, or correct the "architecture rec 4" reference to architecture's "Minimum viable P0" item 5 discussion instead.
2. **Asymmetric treatment of the ChatGPT steelman.** Product-UX scout's §4 explicitly steelmans *both* "the workbench wins" and "raw ChatGPT is good enough for single-shot users, and the gate is friction with no payoff." v0.3 §3.2/§14 carries the workbench-favorable framing clearly and acknowledges ChatGPT serves the job "badly, but served," but never states the ChatGPT-side steelman as crisply as the source scout did (e.g., no explicit "for a single iteration... the gate adds overhead with no visible benefit" sentence). The falsifiable wk-0 test and the "honor a 0/5 result" commitment compensate for this in substance, but a reader comparing v0.3 to `03-product-ux.md` directly would notice the negative case is less prominent.
3. The flat-vector-index-not-graph-DB decision (§8.6, "no separate graph database, since no multi-hop pairing traversal is planned") is a real, traceable fix (matches architecture's over-engineering finding) but isn't called out with its own "Driver:" line in the v0.3 changelog — it's implicitly bundled under the general over-engineering driver. Minor traceability gap, not a grounding problem.
